The History of the Android Mascot

You probably know that Android, the operating system maintained by Google , is one of the most widely used for mobile devices around the world. But did you know that its friendly mascot has a name and a very curious story? Follow this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

First Version

The first attempt to create a mascot came in 2007 and was made by a developer named Dan Morrill . He says he opened Inkscape (a free vector graphics software) and created his own version of a robot. The goal was simply to give the system some personality for his team â€” there was no official request from the company to create a mascot.

This bizarre first version was even named in honor of its creator: they were called the Dandroids.

A New Mascot Emerges

The idea of having a mascot started to evolve, and the task was handed over to a professional in the field. Russian illustrator Irina Blok , who also worked at Google, was given the mission of representing the little robot in a more appealing way.

Irina's main idea was to design everything with simple, flat lines. The drawing also needed to be instantly recognizable and relatable to anyone who looked at it. That's how Bugdroid, the new Android mascot, was born.

The main inspiration for Bugdroid's design came from those little figures used on restroom doors to indicate gender. Legend has it that Irina was sketching at her desk in the Google office, glanced toward the bathrooms, and instantly connected the dots: simple, clean, and to the point.

Want to Learn More?

Another fun fact about Android is that each version was named after a dessert, in alphabetical order, from version 1.5 to 9.0:

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 2.0 / 2.1 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lollipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q never happened, as Google decided to end this fun naming tradition and switched to numbers, starting with Android 10.
